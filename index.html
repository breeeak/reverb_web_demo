Audio Dereverberation demos

Generative adversarial network for music dereverbertion for technical ear training in music production

Manni Chen1, Shuo Meng2, Per Magnus Lindborg1
1School of Creative Media, City University of Hong Kong
2Department of Biomedical Engineering, City University of Hong Kong

Abstract

Reverberation is an audio effect used in music production, which affects spectrum of audio and the timbre of music. In this case, technical ear training on how reverb works on the spectrum of audio is important since sound engineers need to perceive subtle changes in timbre caused by reverb to reach the target texture that they design in music production. In this paper, we propose a deep learning-based dereverberation method, GANreverb, for music production education. The model employs a modified UNet module for the de-reverb task and a GAN-based module acting as a vocoder to generate high-resolution audio. The experiments show that the method can effectively realize dereverberation compared to other neural network-based methods according to various objective evaluation metrics. Moreover, this implementation can be extended to other audio effects like delay and denoising to explore the modifications in terms of technical ear training.
